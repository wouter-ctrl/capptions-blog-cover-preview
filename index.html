Capptions · blog cover system

One generated cover, six motifs, zero cropped photos.

Why generated, not photographic. No source image ever needs cropping, licensing, or sourcing, each cover renders live from the post's title and index.
Palette. Capptions navy + one teal accent, matching the site and the Seveso landing page. No stock-photo drift.

Case studies get their own format

Currently every customer story is just another blog post, same template, same generated cover, no way to tell "we already wrote about Ardagh" from a fresh article. That's structural, not a content problem: it's why the same case study kept reappearing under new slugs. Splitting it into its own type fixes that at the source.

Rotation logic

Each post picks its variant from hash(slug) % 6, so a given article always renders the same cover on rebuild, but the sequence across the blog index feels varied rather than repetitive. Seeded per-post, not per-load, no flicker between visits.

Build notes

Rendered on <canvas>, no external image requests. Respects prefers-reduced-motion (drawn once, static). Same component drives both the blog index card and the post detail hero, fixing the crop bug at the root by removing the raster image entirely.
